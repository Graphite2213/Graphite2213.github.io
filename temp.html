For the longest time ive been racking my brain trying to figure out if my first post on this page should be tag:serbiaposting or about public transit, both topics i could talk about endlessly, so i decided to combine them! Over the past few weeks ive been incredibly annoyed by Belgrade's public transit and promised my friends i'd do this, so if you're one of them, here it goes!

Just some quick background before i dive in:
  • All topics discussed here apply to Belgrade only

  • "GSP" is the name of the Belgrade public transit system, it includes 149 lines

  • "Gangula" is a popular nickname of the current mayor of Belgrade

  • I will cover some topics that were not directly his fault, but rather the fault of his party which is the same thing in my eyes. For the uninitiated, this party has been ruling over Belgrade and Serbia in general for the past 12 years.

  • The way GSP works is a bit confusing: GSP itself owns a bunch of buses, but not all of them. Some lines are covered/provided by private companies, most important of which is Arriva. These have to abide by GSP's rules but get a cut of all fares.

1. EKO Lines

Lines EKO1 and EKO2 were created back in 2016 as an "experiment" to follow the development of the Belgrade Waterfront project (which i'll get into later as a bonus round), and are probably the worst two lines in the city, miles behind the next two on that list, 25P and 3A. They suck because their routes make absolutely no sense, EKO1 stops at a grand total of 16 stops, goes through streets already covered by heaps of lines, and one of its termini is incredibly irrelevant. EKO2 is even worse in this aspect, as its route is between Belgrade Waterfront (we'll get there i promise) and a neighborhood which already has a ton of buses passing through.

Besides that, both of these are so called "short" lines and are served by none other than the Higer KLQ6125GEV3 (ain't that a mouthful, pictured below). These Chinese bad boys are, on god, no cap, 🔥, the worst buses i have ever experienced in my life, even worse than the 30 year old Japanese donation buses (pictured below) still ocasionally roaming the streets of Belgrade. Even in the non-electric version (which is used more prominently, and ill get into them later) sucks, but this one has the added benefit of smelling like ozone due to loose electrical connections. Im genuinely surprised none have caught fire yet.

2. General Neglect

Id be crying if i were a bus, trolleybus or tram forced to work for GSP. These poor things are put through so much, and for what?

Let me prefice this section by saying that there are good, newer buses and trams (but not trolleybuses) that are actually up to modern standards, it's just that they should be the rule not the exception. These are Solaris and BMC double buses, most buses provided by Arriva (especially on lines 33 and 48), and spaniard trams.

A new BMC PROCITY 18M CNG, bought 2023.

Unfortunately if you ever find yourself in Belgrade you are very likely to run into everything else listed in this section, which is not good to say the least. Lets start with the biggest issue of them all: THE BUSES. It's not an uncommon sight these days to see a Japanese donation bus or an old Ikarbus from the 90s roaming the streets. These buses, while rarer these days, still happen sometimes and that's inexcusable for an European capital. There are a few lines like 706 that even exclusively use Ikarbuses. These are old, raggedy and are held together by the mechanics' hopes and dreams.

but at least they don't spontaneously combust.
